Sample complexity of bounded-arity DAG classes and their fan-in-limited MLP realizations

Local arity sets the rate, ambient dimension only logarithmically; fan-in-2 MLPs realize the class exactly.

Encoding & modularity theorysupportedbuilds on: An Exact MLP Encoding of Computation Graphsreproduce: 84d947b · worktree-fan-in-sample-efficiency-plan

TL;DR. For targets computed by finite, axis-aligned, bounded-fan-in computation DAGs (\(N\) lookup-table nodes of local arity \(r\) over alphabet size \(D\) — the class of Note 0), every consistent learner over the matched bounded-arity DAG class reaches 0–1 error \(\epsilon\) from \(n = O\!\big(\tfrac1\epsilon[\,N D^{r}\log D + \sum_v r\log(eM_v/r) + \log(1/\delta)\,]\big)\) samples — table content plus a wiring cost logarithmic in the ambient dimension \(d\), never \(D^{d}\). We prove the full chain (T1–T6: upper bounds, an arity-mismatch floor, a matching \(2^{r} + r\log(d/r)\) lower bound at \(D{=}2\) — forced already by the \(N{=}1\) subclass; matching the \(N\)-dependence remains open — an exact fan-in-2 MLP realization at depth \(O(\Delta\, r\log D)\), and a quantized capacity separation), and an exact-enumeration campaign illustrates the predicted finite-sample signatures: ambient dimension nearly free (paired \(n(0.1)\) ratio \(1.25\) for \(d\,{=}\,6{\to}12\) against a \(514\times\) flat-class wall), the version-space error U-shape minimized at \(k=r\), and GF(2) basis-mixing collapsing the advantage (median error \(0.404\) vs \(0.090\)). theory: supported  signatures: supported

1. Motivation

The program this study belongs to asks when per-neuron structure buys statistical efficiency. Note 0 settled the representation side: every finite axis-aligned computation DAG — hidden nodes computing lookup tables \(z_v = g_v(z_{\mathrm{pa}(v)})\) of fan-in at most \(r\) over alphabet-\(D\) variables — embeds exactly into an MLP. A companion foundations note pinned the complementary constraint: sample efficiency is a property of the function class, blind to the neuron basis chosen to represent it. Together they leave one question open, and it is this study's: does the bounded-arity DAG class itself have small statistical complexity — small enough to escape the \(D^{d}\) flat-function wall, cheap in the unknown-wiring case, minimized at the true arity, and realizable by architectures whose per-neuron fan-in is bounded?

The study is a theory note first and an experiment second, and its verdict keeps that order (§8): the theorems of §3 stand or fall with their proofs alone, while the exact-enumeration campaign of §4–§7 illustrates the finite-sample signatures the theory predicts and stress-tests its modeling assumptions, with SGD removed as a confound. Hypotheses, cells, thresholds, seeds, and statistics were locked before any data existed (plan/contract.json, status locked, 2026-07-04); one amendment (A01, locked after the adversarial proof audit but still before dispatch, so before any campaign data) re-pinned theorem statements only — no cell, threshold, seed, or statistic changed. Everything below is pre-registered unless labeled descriptive; theorems are stated in their post-amendment (A01) form.

2. Setting

The class. All variables share a finite alphabet of size \(D\); inputs are \(x \in \Sigma^{d}\). A target is computed by a DAG over the \(d\) input coordinates and \(N\) named internal nodes with one designated output node: internal node \(v\) computes \(z_v = g_v(z_{\mathrm{pa}(v)})\) by an arbitrary lookup table over its \(r_v \le r\) parents (the fan-in, or local arity, is \(r = \max_v r_v\)); \(\Delta\) is the DAG depth. \(\mathcal F_G\) denotes the functions computable with structure \(G\) fixed and tables free; the fan-in-\(r\) class is the union over admissible structures, where node \(v\) draws its parents from a candidate set of size \(M_v \le d + N\). Axis-aligned means the DAG's variables are literal input coordinates and named latent nodes — the class is defined in a basis.

Learning is realizable 0–1 classification on the output symbol: the target \(f^\star\) lies in the class, inputs are i.i.d. \(x \sim P\), and the learner sees \(S_n = \{(x_i, f^\star(x_i))\}_{i=1}^{n}\). T1/T2/T5 are distribution-free; T3's floor and all experiments fix \(P\) uniform. In the campaign, test error is always the exact expectation over all \(D^{d}\) inputs — never a sampled test set. (Agnostic remark, not used anywhere: for a finite class, uniform convergence gives the standard \(n = O(\epsilon^{-2}[\ln|\mathcal H| + \ln(1/\delta)])\) rate.)

Two learner notions are kept explicit throughout. The theorems hold for every consistent learner (ERM) — any rule returning a hypothesis with zero empirical error. The experiments run the uniform version-space (VS) predictor: predict with a uniform draw from the version space (the hypotheses consistent with \(S_n\)) — a Gibbs-style learner, not deterministic ERM — under two pre-registered weightings: w-rep, uniform over (graph, tables) descriptions (functions with many encodings get extra weight), and w-fun, uniform over distinct input–output maps (function-deduplicated via truth-table hashing, or closed forms at \(N{=}1\)). Gates evaluate on w-fun; w-rep is the pre-registered sensitivity, and divergence between the two would itself be a reported finding. When the version space is empty — the deliberately non-realizable regimes: \(k \lt r\) arms, basis-mixing, label-shuffle — the predictor draws uniformly from the minimum-empirical-disagreement set and is then named the uniform-min-ERM predictor (there is no version space).

A standing remark the contract carries verbatim: “the finite-class upper bounds count descriptions, so they remain valid under duplicate encodings of the same function; function-deduplication only tightens the empirical VS predictor.” The campaign measured the slack: the diamond class at \(d{=}9\) has \(49{,}545{,}216\) descriptions computing only \(376{,}700\) distinct functions — \(99.24\%\) duplicates (\(d{=}6\): \(5{,}898{,}240 \to 37{,}484\), \(99.36\%\)).

3. Theory

“Fully proved” (Gate A, §8) means complete proofs in this note modulo exactly one citation-grade import: [B] Bartlett, Harvey, Liaw & Mehrabian, JMLR 20(63), 2019 — VC/pseudo-dimension \(\tilde O(WL)\) for piecewise-linear networks — used only in T5(c)'s labeled real-valued remark. (A junta-VC import was pre-authorized and went unused: both shattering directions of T4 are self-proved.) Each theorem passed a fresh-context adversarial proof-check before the campaign was dispatched; the statement re-pinnings that audit forced are amendment A01, and the statements below are the A01 ones. The full proof text lives beside this report (report/theory_proofs.md) and is reproduced here in condensed but complete form.

3.1 Known structure

Lemma 1 (finite-class realizable bound). Let \(\mathcal H\) be finite with \(f^\star \in \mathcal H\), and let \(\hat h \in \mathcal H\) be any hypothesis consistent with \(S_n\). If

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:l1} n \;\ge\; \tfrac1\epsilon\Big(\ln|\mathcal H| + \ln\tfrac1\delta\Big), \end{equation}\]

then with probability at least \(1-\delta\), \(\mathrm{err}_P(\hat h) \le \epsilon\).

Proof. Fix \(h\) with \(\mathrm{err}_P(h) \gt \epsilon\): each sample is consistent with \(h\) with probability below \(1-\epsilon\), so \(h\) survives all \(n\) with probability below \((1-\epsilon)^{n} \le e^{-\epsilon n}\) (from \(1+x \le e^{x}\)). A union bound over the at most \(|\mathcal H|\) such \(h\) makes the survival probability of any \(\epsilon\)-bad hypothesis below \(|\mathcal H|\,e^{-\epsilon n} \le \delta\) at \eqref{eq:l1}; a consistent \(\hat h\) exists by realizability and is then \(\epsilon\)-good. \(\square\) No \(\log(1/\epsilon)\) factor appears; the bound is the plain \(\epsilon^{-1}(\ln|\mathcal H| + \ln(1/\delta))\) form, derived, not imported.

Lemma 2 (counting, known DAG). For a fixed graph \(G\) with node arities \(r_v \le r\),

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:l2} \log_2|\mathcal F_G| \;\le\; \sum_{v} D^{r_v}\log_2 D \;\le\; N\,D^{r}\log_2 D. \end{equation}\]

Proof. A description fixes each node's table (\(\prod_v D^{D^{r_v}}\) choices); functions are at most descriptions. \(\square\)

Theorem T1 (known DAG). Every consistent learner over \(\mathcal F_G\) reaches error \(\epsilon\) with probability at least \(1-\delta\) from \(n = O\big(\tfrac1\epsilon[\,N D^{r}\log D + \log(1/\delta)\,]\big)\) samples. Proof. Lemma 1 + Lemma 2. \(\square\)

3.2 Unknown wiring

Lemma 3 (counting, unknown DAG — scope pinned). Fix the node set (count \(N\), labels), the designated output, the alphabets, and candidate-parent sets of sizes \(r \le M_v \le d+N\) with \(\mathrm{pa}^\star(v) \subseteq\) the candidates for every node (realizability hypothesis). Let \(\mathcal F\) be all functions computed by choices of parent sets of size at most \(r\) drawn from the candidates (acyclic) together with tables. Then

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:l3} \log_2|\mathcal F| \;\le\; \sum_v\Big[r\log_2\tfrac{eM_v}{r} + D^{r}\log_2 D\Big] \;=\; N D^{r}\log_2 D + O\!\Big(N r\log\tfrac{e(d+N)}{r}\Big). \end{equation}\]

Proof. Every \(f \in \mathcal F\) has, by definition, an acyclic description with parent sets of size at most \(r\) — no padding needed — so \(|\mathcal F|\) is at most the number of all (parent-sets, tables) tuples, acyclic or not (dropping acyclicity only enlarges the count): \(|\mathcal F| \le \prod_v \big[\sum_{j \le r}\binom{M_v}{j}\big] D^{D^{r}}\), where a fan-in-\(j\) table with \(j \lt r\) is subsumed by \(D^{D^{j}} \le D^{D^{r}}\). The binomial sum is bounded in two lines: for \(M \ge r\), multiplying term \(j\) by \((r/M)^{j-r} \ge 1\) gives

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:binom} \sum_{j\le r}\binom{M}{j} \;\le\; \Big(\tfrac{M}{r}\Big)^{r}\sum_{j\le M}\binom{M}{j} \Big(\tfrac{r}{M}\Big)^{j} \;=\; \Big(\tfrac{M}{r}\Big)^{r}\Big(1+\tfrac{r}{M}\Big)^{M} \;\le\; \Big(\tfrac{eM}{r}\Big)^{r}\qquad (M \ge r), \end{equation}\]

using \(1+x \le e^{x}\); this is where \(M_v \ge r\) is used (for \(M_v \lt r\) it can fail, and the study never needs that regime — full candidate sets have \(M_v = d+N-1 \ge r\) and the diamond leaf pairs have \(M_v = d \ge r\)). Because the relaxed count never uses a topological order, unknown order is covered for free when the candidate sets are order-free (\(M_v\) = all other nodes + inputs); with narrower candidate sets the order-coverage claim is vacuous rather than wrong. Unknown \(N\), unknown output designation, and unknown alphabets are not covered and not claimed. \(\square\)

Theorem T2 (unknown DAG). Every consistent learner over Lemma 3's class reaches error \(\epsilon\) with probability at least \(1-\delta\) from

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:t2} n \;=\; O\!\Big(\tfrac1\epsilon\Big[N D^{r}\log D + \sum_v r\log\tfrac{eM_v}{r} + \log\tfrac1\delta\Big]\Big) \end{equation}\]

samples. The graph-selection term is \(O(N\,r\log(e(d+N)/r))\) — logarithmic in \(d\); the ambient dimension never enters exponentially. Proof. Lemma 1 + Lemma 3. \(\square\)

3.3 Arity mismatch

Theorem T3 (monotone cost above, floor below — scope pinned). (a) For \(k \ge r\): the fan-in-\(\le k\) class contains the fan-in-\(\le r\) class outright, so realizability transfers unconditionally; if additionally \(\min_v M_v \ge k\) (every campaign cell satisfies it, \(M_v = d \ge k\)), T2 applies at arity \(k\):

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:t3k} n \;=\; O\!\Big(\tfrac1\epsilon\Big[N D^{k}\log D + \sum_v k\log\tfrac{eM_v}{k} + \log\tfrac1\delta\Big]\Big), \end{equation}\]

monotone increasing in \(k\) on the admissible range \(k \le \min_v M_v\). (b) For \(k \lt r\), an approximation floor — for the node-matched single-node class (\(N{=}1\): \(k\)-juntas \(\mathcal J_k\), \(D{=}2\), uniform inputs, exact-arity essential targets):

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:floor} \inf_{h\in\mathcal J_k}\mathrm{err}(h;f) \;\ge\; 2^{-r}\ \ \text{for every essential exact-arity-}r\ f,\qquad\text{hence}\quad c_{r,k} \;\ge\; 2^{-r} \;\gt\; 0, \end{equation}\]

where \(c_{r,k}\) is the expected best-in-class error over uniform essential targets. At \(k{=}1\) the floor holds at any \(N\) (fan-in-1 compositions are 1-juntas at any size). The intermediate case — fixed node set, \(N \ge 2\), \(2 \le k \lt r\) — is declared open: no cell tests it and nothing downstream depends on it.

The floor cannot hold for the unrestricted-\(N\) fan-in-\(k\) class once \(k \ge 2\): 2-ary tables include AND/OR/NOT, so fan-in-2 table-DAGs with enough nodes compute every Boolean function (two-input-gate universality — indeed T5(a)'s own construction is literally a fan-in-2 Boolean DAG). That is why A01 pinned the floor to the node-matched class — which is exactly the class the arity-sweep cells always enumerated.

Proof of \eqref{eq:floor}. Flip Lemma: for \(D{=}2\), \(P\) uniform, and \(h\) not depending on coordinate \(i\), \(\mathrm{err}(h;f) \ge \tfrac12\mathrm{Inf}_i(f)\) where \(\mathrm{Inf}_i(f) = \Pr_x[f(x) \ne f(x \oplus e_i)]\): pair the inputs \(\{x, x\oplus e_i\}\); on a pair where \(f\) differs, \(h\) (equal on both) errs on at least one of the two equally likely points. Any \(h \in \mathcal J_k\) with \(k \lt r\) ignores some essential parent \(i\); essentiality gives at least one differing pair among the \(2^{r-1}\) co-assignments of the other parents, and the \(d-r\) non-parents are dummies, so \(\mathrm{Inf}_i(f) \ge 2^{-(r-1)}\). Under arbitrary \(P\) no distribution-free floor exists (an \(r\)-ary target can look \(k\)-ary on the support), which is why the theorem pins \(P\) uniform. \(\square\)

Anchor, computed exactly. At \((r,k) = (2,1)\), \(D{=}2\): the 16 two-bit tables split into 6 non-essential and 10 essential; a best 1-junta (constants included) errs \(1/4\) on each of the 8 AND/OR-type essential functions and \(1/2\) on XOR/XNOR, so \(c_{2,1} = (8\cdot\tfrac14 + 2\cdot\tfrac12)/10 = 0.3\) exactly. The campaign's analysis re-verified this (10 essential tables, mean best-1-junta error \(0.3\), exact match — §6). The adversarial pass additionally pinned \(c_{3,2} = 43/218 \approx 0.197\) and \(c_{3,1} = 55/218 \approx 0.252\), both above the \(2^{-r}\) floor, which is pointwise tight.

Corollary (\(k=r\) minimality — wording part of the contract, scoped per A01). Among arities \(k \ge r\) the finite-class upper bound \eqref{eq:t3k} is minimized at \(k = r\) — unconditional, by T3(a) monotonicity. That \(k = r\) is moreover the smallest realizable arity is established at \(N{=}1\) by the floor \eqref{eq:floor} (and at \(k{=}1\) for any \(N\)); realizability at \(N \ge 2\), \(2 \le k \lt r\) is the declared open case. This is a statement about the bound, not a universal statistical theorem.

The finite-sample right arm — \(k \gt r\) hurting at fixed \(n\) — is the over-classing effect of the uniform-VS predictor (larger \(k\) leaves more spurious consistent hypotheses), not a property of ERM in general. The Occam-ERM that returns a consistent hypothesis of minimal per-node essential fan-in inside \(\mathcal F_k \supseteq \mathcal F_r\) collapses the right arm exactly: realizability puts \(f^\star\) (per-node essential fan-in \(\le r\)) in the consistent set, so the minimal consistent output has essential fan-in \(\le r\); pruning inessential parents exhibits it in \(\mathcal F_r\), and Lemma 1 applied to \(\mathcal F_r\) gives exactly T2-at-\(r\) for every \(k \ge r\) — the rule's output set is \(k\)-invariant, so its right arm is exactly flat. (Caveat pinned: “essential arity” means per-node essential fan-in; under the composed-function reading the pruning step fails for \(N \gt 1\); at \(N{=}1\), where the U-shape lives, the two readings coincide.) The right arm as a theorem for the VS predictor (T3′) is a pre-registered high-risk stretch that was not attempted, per its pre-commitment; nothing depends on it (§5.2 reports its numeric probe).

3.4 Lower bound at \(D=2\)

Theorem T4 (junta subclass). There is a constant \(c \gt 0\) such that any learner (possibly randomized, possibly improper) that, for every distribution \(P\) on \(\{0,1\}^{d}\) and every target in the fan-in-\(r\) class, achieves error at most \(\epsilon\) with probability at least \(1-\delta\) (hypotheses: \(\epsilon \le 1/8\), \(\delta \le 1/8\), \(d \ge 2r\)) requires

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:t4} n \;\ge\; c\,\frac{2^{r} + r\log_2(d/r) + \log_2(1/\delta)}{\epsilon}. \end{equation}\]

Already the \(N{=}1\) junta subclass forces this; the \(N\)-linear upper-bound term of T1/T2 is not matched from below, and that gap is left open. (The for-every-\(P\) quantifier matches the distribution-free upper bounds.)

Proof. Since the \(r\)-junta class \(\mathcal J_r\) is contained in the fan-in-\(r\) target class (\(N{=}1\)), a minimax lower bound for \(\mathcal J_r\) lower-bounds the larger problem. Both shattering directions are self-contained. VC \(\ge 2^{r}\): fix a support \(S_0\), \(|S_0| = r\); the \(2^{r}\) inputs that are zero off \(S_0\) are shattered by the tables on \(S_0\). VC \(\ge r\lfloor\log_2\lfloor d/r\rfloor\rfloor\) (block-dictator construction, \(d \ge 2r\)): partition the \(d\) coordinates into \(r\) blocks of size \(q = \lfloor d/r\rfloor\) and set \(m_b = \lfloor\log_2 q\rfloor \ge 1\). In block \(b\) pick \(m_b\) points, each supported on block \(b\) only, whose \(q\)-column patterns over the block's coordinates realize all \(2^{m_b}\) binary columns (possible since \(2^{m_b} \le q\)). Given any labeling \(y\), choose in each block the coordinate \(j_b\) whose column equals \(y\) restricted to that block, and set \(f = \bigoplus_b x_{j_b}\) — an XOR of \(r\) dictators, hence an \(r\)-junta. On a block-\(b\) point all other blocks' chosen coordinates read 0, so \(f\) equals the required label: the set is shattered.

(i) \(\Omega(m/\epsilon)\) from a shattered set of size \(m \ge 2\). Define \(P\): \(P(x_0) = 1-8\epsilon\), \(P(x_j) = 8\epsilon/(m-1)\) for \(j \ge 1\); draw the target uniformly among the \(2^{m-1}\) shattered labelings fixing \(f(x_0)\). Suppose \(n \le (m-1)/(64\epsilon)\). Each rare point is unseen with probability \((1 - \tfrac{8\epsilon}{m-1})^{n} \ge 1 - \tfrac{8\epsilon n}{m-1} \ge \tfrac78\), so the expected number unseen is at least \(\tfrac78(m-1)\). Define the rare-mass error

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:raremass} X \;=\; \tfrac{8\epsilon}{m-1}\,\#\{j \ge 1 : \hat h(x_j) \ne f(x_j)\} \;\in\; [0, 8\epsilon], \qquad X \le \mathrm{err}_P(\hat h). \end{equation}\]

On an unseen rare point the target label is uniform and independent of the learner's (even randomized) prediction, so it is mispredicted with probability exactly \(\tfrac12\); hence \(\mathbb E X \ge \tfrac12\cdot\tfrac{8\epsilon}{m-1}\cdot\tfrac78(m-1) = 3.5\,\epsilon\) jointly over target, sample, and learner randomness. Reverse Markov on \(X \in [0, 8\epsilon]\): \(\Pr[X \gt \epsilon] \ge (\mathbb E X - \epsilon)/(7\epsilon) \ge \tfrac{5}{14} \gt \tfrac18 \ge \delta\). Averaging over targets, some fixed target fails with probability above \(\delta\) — so \(n \gt (m-1)/(64\epsilon)\), and \(m-1 \ge m/2\). (ii) \(\Omega(\log(1/\delta)/\epsilon)\). Two shattered points: \(P(x_0) = 1-2\epsilon\), \(P(x_1) = 2\epsilon\); shattering supplies \(f_0, f_1\) agreeing at \(x_0\), differing at \(x_1\). With \(E\) = “\(x_1\) unseen”, \(\Pr[E] = (1-2\epsilon)^{n}\); for \(\epsilon \le 1/8\), \(-\ln(1-2\epsilon) \le 0.576\cdot 4\epsilon\), so at \(n \le \ln(1/\delta)/(4\epsilon)\), \(\Pr[E] \ge \delta^{0.576}\). On \(E\) the sample is identical under both targets, so the prediction at \(x_1\) errs against one of them with conditional probability at least \(\tfrac12\), costing error \(2\epsilon \gt \epsilon\); hence \(\max_i \Pr_{f_i}[\mathrm{err} \gt \epsilon] \ge \tfrac12\delta^{0.576} \ge 1.2\,\delta \gt \delta\) for \(\delta \le \tfrac18\). Combining (i) at \(m = \max(2^{r},\, r\lfloor\log_2\lfloor d/r\rfloor\rfloor)\) with (ii) via \(\max(a,b) \ge \tfrac12(a+b)\) gives \eqref{eq:t4}. \(\square\)

Consequence. At \(D{=}2\) the \(k{=}r\) consistent learner of T2 is minimax-near-optimal in the leading \(2^{r}\) and \(r\log(d/r)\) dependencies (up to constants). “Sample-optimal” is deliberately not claimed beyond this; nothing is claimed for \(D \gt 2\), and the top-level claim's lower-bound clause reads “at \(D{=}2\) (i.e. \(2^{r}\))” for exactly this reason.

3.5 The MLP bridge

Input-encoding convention, part of every T5 statement: the input layer is the one-hot encoding of the \(d\) symbolic variables (one group of \(D\) coordinates per variable); scalar fan-in \(k\) counts one-hot coordinates, DAG arity \(r\) counts variable groups — the two are never conflated. (The scalar-symbol alternative must first build equality indicators and pay their units, depth, and fan-in; it is a remark, not the route taken.)

Theorem T5(a) (realizability at \(k=2\) with depth inflation). Every computation-graph function (arities \(r_v \le r\), depth \(\Delta\), common alphabet \(D\)) is computed exactly by a ReLU network of scalar fan-in at most 2, integer weights of magnitude at most 1 with biases in \(\{0, -1\}\) (\(b = 2\) bits per parameter), whose output is a designated one-hot group inside the network (no external argmax), with

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:t5size} \begin{aligned} \text{depth} \;&\le\; \Delta\big(\lceil\log_2\max(r,2)\rceil + \lceil r\log_2 D\rceil + 1\big) \ \ \text{ReLU layers},\\ \#\text{units} \;&=\; O\!\big(N D^{r} r + N D\,\Delta\, r^{2}\log D\big), \end{aligned} \end{equation}\]

where the \(r^{2}\) term carries the live-input factor \(\min(d, Nr)\).

Proof. Maintain the invariant (I1) that after processing DAG level \(\ell\), every still-needed node \(u\) owns a group of units whose activations form exactly the one-hot \(\xi_u = e_{z_u} \in \{0,1\}^{D}\); level 0 is the input encoding. For node \(v\) with parents \(p_1 \dots p_{r_v}\): conjunction trees — for each parent-value row \(c\), AND the \(r_v\) bits \(\xi_{p_j}[c_j]\) pairwise with \(\mathrm{AND}(a,b) = \mathrm{ReLU}(a+b-1)\) (exact on \(\{0,1\}\)), reaching the row indicator \(h^v_c\) after \(\lceil\log_2 r_v\rceil\) layers; because each parent group is one-hot, exactly one row unit is active per input. OR trees — for each output symbol \(s\), sum the row units of \(g_v^{-1}(s)\) pairwise with \(\mathrm{SUM}(a,b) = \mathrm{ReLU}(a+b)\); since at most one summand is ever 1 (rows mutually exclusive), all partial sums stay in \(\{0,1\}\) (I3), and after at most \(\lceil r\log_2 D\rceil\) layers the group \(\xi_v\) is one-hot again (I1 restored). All nodes of a level run their trees in parallel inside shared physical layers, padding shorter trees and still-needed groups with identity units \(\mathrm{ReLU}(x)\) (fan-in 1) — so depth is per-level, never per-node (I5), with \(+1\) slack covering unary leaf-copy stages. Every unit has scalar fan-in at most 2 (I2) and integer weights \(|w| \le 1\), biases in \(\{0,-1\}\). Units: node \(v\) costs \(O(D^{r_v} r_v)\) tree units (the first AND stage dominates, then geometric decay, plus \(D\) OR roots); carries cost \(O(\text{live groups}\cdot D)\) per physical layer, and live groups never exceed \(N + \min(d, Nr)\) (only hidden nodes and consumed inputs are carried), so carries total \(O\big((N + \min(d, Nr))\,D\,\Delta\,r\log D\big) \subseteq O(N D\,\Delta\,r^{2}\log D)\) — the carried-input factor is not \(O(N)\) alone and is stated explicitly. The output node's group is the network's last-layer one-hot (I4). \(\square\)

Executable witness (part of Gate A). src/verify_t5.py builds exactly this construction and asserts, on 80 random graphs (\(D \in \{2,3\}\), \(r \in \{1,2,3\}\), \(N \in \{1,\dots,4\}\), mixed arities with unary nodes, plus the campaign's diamond and chain): exact I/O equality on all \(D^{d}\) inputs, max scalar fan-in \(\le 2\), integer weights \(|w| \le 1\) with biases in \(\{0,-1\}\), the depth bound of \eqref{eq:t5size}, and one-hotness of every carried group at every level boundary. All 80 pass; unit counts are reported, not asserted.

Theorem T5(b) (fan-in-1 impossibility — output mechanism pinned). (i) If every unit including the designated output unit has fan-in at most 1, then by induction every unit's activation is a function of at most one input coordinate; the output therefore cannot equal any target essential on 2 or more coordinates. (ii) Relaxed variant — unrestricted linear readout, or argmax over scores, over fan-in-1 features: every feature depends on at most one coordinate, so each score is additive across single-coordinate functions; for two scores, argmax is the sign of an additive score against a threshold. For XOR (\(D{=}2\)): \(s(00) + s(11) = s(01) + s(10)\), hence \(\min(s_{01}, s_{10}) \le \tfrac12(s_{01}+s_{10}) = \tfrac12(s_{00}+s_{11}) \le \max(s_{00}, s_{11})\) — correctness would need the strict opposite under either tie rule. Additive scores cannot decide XOR, so generic \(r \ge 2\)-ary interactions are excluded in both variants. \(\square\)

Theorem T5(‌c) (capacity — quantized vs equally-quantized, never mixed). The class \(\mathcal F_{k,b}\) of ReLU networks with architecture \(\{m_\ell\}_{\ell=1}^{L}\), per-unit fan-in at most \(k\), and integer parameters in \(b\) bits satisfies

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:cap} \log_2|\mathcal F_{k,b}| \;\le\; \sum_{\ell} m_\ell\Big[k\log_2\tfrac{e\,m_{\ell-1}}{k} + (k+1)\,b\Big] \end{equation}\]

(each unit: a \(\le k\)-subset of the previous layer — subsumed by exactly-\(k\) subsets with zero weights, \(\binom{m_{\ell-1}}{k} \le (e m_{\ell-1}/k)^{k}\) by \eqref{eq:binom} — plus \(k\) weights and a bias at \(b\) bits; the output group is WLOG the first \(D\) units of the last layer). The equally-quantized dense class at the same architecture and \(b\) has description count \(\Theta\big(b\sum_\ell m_\ell(m_{\ell-1}+1)\big) = \Theta(\Delta' m^{2} b)\) at width \(m\), depth \(\Delta'\) (functions at most descriptions; the function-count lower bound is not claimed). At T5(a)'s realizing sizes — \(b = 2\), \(\sum_\ell m_\ell = \tilde O(N D^{r})\) under the explicit regime hypothesis \(\Delta = O(r D^{r-1})\) (Note 0's regime, which absorbs the carry term), with \(\tilde O\) hiding \(\mathrm{polylog}(N D^{r})\) — the fan-in-2 class costs \(\log_2|\mathcal F| = \tilde O(N r D^{r})\) against the dense description count \(\Theta(\Delta' m^{2} b)\) with \(m = \tilde O(N D^{r})\): near-linear in \(N D^{r}\) versus quadratic in the width, at equal architecture, both sides quantized at the same \(b\). Real-valued remark (separate; the import [B]): sparse real-valued ReLU networks with \(W\) weights and \(L\) layers have VC/pseudo-dimension \(\tilde O(WL)\); with \(W = k\sum_\ell m_\ell\) this is the real-valued analogue and is never combined with the counting bound in one inequality.

Theorem T5(d) (flat comparison). Arbitrary \(f : \Sigma^{d} \to \Sigma\) form a class of \(\log_2\)-size \(D^{d}\log_2 D\); T2's class costs \(N D^{r}\log_2 D + O(N r\log\frac{e(d+N)}{r})\) — exponentially separated once \(r \ll d\) and \(N = \mathrm{poly}(d)\). \(\square\)

3.6 A bad ERM on the flat class

Theorem T6 (bad-ERM separation — stretch, included). There exists an ERM over the flat class needing \(n = \Omega(D^{d})\) samples to reach error at most \(\tfrac14(1-1/D)\) on a fan-in-\(r\) realizable target under uniform inputs, while by T1/T2 every consistent learner over the matched class needs only \(\tilde O(N D^{r})/\epsilon\).

Proof. The adversarial consistent rule \(\hat h\): on seen inputs predict the seen label; on every unseen input predict \(f^\star(x) + 1 \pmod D\). It is an ERM for the flat class — the returned hypothesis is in the class with empirical risk exactly 0; the ERM principle does not forbid this tie-break, which is the point: the principle alone gives no rate for the flat class. The rule is wrong on every unseen input, and \(n\) samples cover at most \(n\) distinct inputs, so

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:t6} \mathrm{err}(\hat h) \;\ge\; 1 - n\,D^{-d}\qquad\text{deterministically}; \end{equation}\]

error at most \(\tfrac14(1-1/D) \le \tfrac14\) forces \(n \ge \tfrac34 D^{d}\) on every realization. \(\square\)

4. The enumeration campaign

The campaign's role is the one §1 fixed: sanity checks and mechanism evidence, never the logical foundation (§8). Exact enumeration over the discrete classes removes SGD as a confound; it cannot and does not validate SGD-trained MLP behavior (out of scope, §9).

Learners. L-flat: all functions on \(\Sigma^{d}\) (its VS error has a closed form, \eqref{eq:flat} below). L-known: true graph, tables unknown. L-unk: fan-in-\(r\) structures + tables; in the \(N \ge 2\) cells the wiring shape is known and the input assignment + tables are unknown (recorded realization, mirroring the diamond restriction below). L-k for \(k \in \{1,2,3,4\}\): the fan-in-\(k\) class (at \(N{=}1\), \(k\)-juntas with per-mask closed forms). L-tables-oracle: true tables, graph unknown — the complementary oracle. L-unk-diamond: wiring shape and \(x_e\) known, the two disjoint leaf pairs and all tables unknown. Every learner is the VS predictor in both weightings where feasible, with the empty-VS rule of §2.

Cells. Eleven cells at \(D{=}2\) unless stated, 20 target draws each (50 for the arity sweep):

The compositional diamond is the \(N{=}4\) branching-and-merging family

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:diamond} z_1 = g_1(x_a, x_b),\quad z_2 = g_2(x_c, x_d),\quad z_3 = g_3(z_1, z_2),\quad y = g_4(z_3, x_e), \end{equation}\]

with \(a,b,c,d,e\) pairwise distinct (targets violating this are rejected). The learner's skeleton class deliberately remains \(e\)-inclusive — leaf pairs drawn from all \(d\) coordinates, a stated superclass (this is what the 90/756 skeleton counts enumerate) — and the \(x_e\)-known restriction is an enumeration-cost concession. Targets are drawn uniformly conditioned on global essentiality: every local table uses every parent, the composed truth table has every intended input observationally essential, and (for \(N \ge 2\)) every internal node has interventional influence on \(y\) — the two criteria are deliberately different, observational for inputs, interventional for latent nodes, the cleaner choice there. Rejection rates are recorded in the shards. Pairing: each outer seed draws its essential core (wiring + tables) once and embeds it at every \(d\) in the grid with canonical parent coordinates (class permutation-symmetry + uniform inputs make the embedding choice immaterial), so every cross-\(d\) comparison below is a within-target paired ratio or gap.

Sample-complexity readout. Errors are evaluated on the grid \(n \in \{8, 16, 32, 64, 128, 256, 512, 1024, 2048, 4096\}\); \(n_i(\epsilon)\) is the crossing of target \(i\)'s log-linearly interpolated error curve with \(\epsilon = 0.1\). Wherever a gated crossing-ratio prediction's crossing (P1, P6a; §5.1) fell between grid points with \(n\)-ratio above \(\sqrt2\), one integer-rounded log-midpoint was inserted into that gap — the exact \(\sqrt2\) point in log space, though rounding to integer sample counts leaves the realized bracketing ratio at \(\le 1.46\) rather than exactly \(\le \sqrt2\) (inserted: \(n = 11\) for P1, \(n = 23, 45\) for P6a; identical grids across each prediction's compared cells, logged in shard metadata); no other densification, and the gated ratios are insensitive to the residual at their observed margins (\(1.25\) against threshold \(2.0\), walls \(514\times\)/\(27\times\) against \(3\); §5.1). The U-shape's reference point (P3, §5.2) was instead read on the base grid — \(n^\ast\) is the integer-rounded median of L-k=2's interpolated crossings (\(12.30 \to 12\); the enriched-grid median \(11.64\) rounds to the same value) — then inserted as an exact evaluation grid point, so the error readouts at \(n^\ast\) (P3, and later P4 and M3) are measured directly at \(n = 12\), not interpolated. Censoring (pre-registered): a curve not crossing by \(n_{\max} = 4096\) reports \(n(\epsilon) \gt 4096\) (right-censored; ratios involving it become bounds, and a flat-wall clause passes if the censored bound already exceeds its threshold); a curve already below \(\epsilon\) at \(n_{\min} = 8\) reports \(n(\epsilon) \lt 8\) (left-censored; bounds propagate in the direction unfavorable to passing), and a gate undecidable on censored intervals is reported “not resolved at these scales”, never silently passed. The flat class needs no enumeration: under w-fun its VS predictor guesses each unseen input uniformly (wrong with probability \(1 - 1/D\)), giving the closed form

\[\begin{equation}\label{eq:flat} \mathbb E\,\mathrm{err}_n(\text{L-flat}) = \Big(1-\tfrac1D\Big)\big(1 - D^{-d}\big)^{n}, \qquad n_{\text{flat}}(\epsilon) = \frac{\ln\!\big(\epsilon/(1-\tfrac1D)\big)}{\ln(1 - D^{-d})}. \end{equation}\]

Statistics. Intervals are bootstrap 95% percentile CIs over target draws (10,000 seeded resamples; the resampling unit is the target draw). Tests are one-sided exact-binomial sign tests on within-target paired differences (ties dropped). Gated ratio/threshold clauses are CI-aware: they pass only if the 95% CI excludes the violating side. Replication: 20 target draws per cell (outer seeds 0–19; 50, seeds 0–49, for the arity sweep); 50 nested sample draws per (target, \(n\)) — prefixes of one master stream per (target, draw); the VS predictor's own randomness is integrated exactly wherever closed forms exist (all \(N{=}1\) cells and L-flat) and folded into 64 tie draws otherwise (\(N \ge 2\) cells). Reported curves are medians over targets of per-target means over sample draws. There is no training loop: every learner is a parameter-free exact procedure over a pre-registered class with the identical prediction rule and empty-VS fallback, so there are no hyperparameters anywhere and HP-trial parity is a justified omission. All randomness derives from seed = SHA256(cell_id, target_seed, n, draw_idx) & 0xFFFFFFFF, recorded in the shards.

Pilot and execution. The mandatory feasibility pilot ran the worst planned cell (diamond, unknown leaves, \(d{=}9\): 756 skeletons, \(49{,}545{,}216\) descriptions, asserted under the \(6\times10^{7}\) runtime cap) and measured setup cost 21.6 s, marginal cost 8.3 ms per (target × sample-draw × \(n\)-point), projected full cell cost 0.03 h against the 8 h cap and peak RSS 0.07 GB against the 16 GB cap — pass on both, so the whole campaign ran in-process on CPU (0 SLURM jobs) and the pre-registered downgrade cascade was never engaged: every cell, including the diamond at \(d{=}9\), ran at full pre-registered scope. Descriptions were streamed in chunks against bit-packed truth tables with precomputed sample masks; per-cell duplicate rates were recorded (§2).

5.1 d-flatness

5.2 The over-classing U-shape

P3 (gating; w-fun arm, 50 targets). Let \(\mathrm{err}_i(k)\) be target \(i\)'s exact error of L-k at the cell's pre-registered reference point \(n^\ast\) — L-k=2's median interpolated crossing \(n(0.1)\), which the campaign fixed at \(n^\ast = 12\) and evaluated as an exact grid point (§4). Clauses: \(\mathrm{median}_i[\mathrm{err}_i(1) - \mathrm{err}_i(2)] \ge 0.05\) and \(\mathrm{median}_i[\mathrm{err}_i(3) - \mathrm{err}_i(2)] \ge 0.02\), each with a one-sided sign test on (difference − margin) at \(p \lt 0.05\); \(\mathrm{median}_i[\mathrm{err}_i(4) - \mathrm{err}_i(3)] \ge 0\); and at \(r{=}3\), median error minimal at \(k{=}3\) over \(k \in \{2,3,4\}\). Result: pass. Median errors at \(n^\ast\) are \(0.282,\ 0.090,\ 0.237,\ 0.316\) for \(k = 1,2,3,4\) — the U bottoms exactly at \(k = r = 2\) (Figure 3). The paired differences are decisive: \(0.191\) (\(k{=}1\) vs 2) and \(0.147\) (\(k{=}3\) vs 2), with every one of the 50 targets clearing its margin (sign tests \(p = 2^{-50} \approx 9\times10^{-16}\)); the right arm keeps rising, \(0.079\) (\(k{=}4\) vs 3). At \(r{=}3\) the medians are \(0.298, 0.249, 0.349\) for \(k = 2,3,4\) — minimal at \(k = r = 3\). The w-rep sensitivity arm agrees (\(0.275, 0.094, 0.204, 0.313\)): no parameterization-dependence to report.

M3 — U-shape anatomy (descriptive). The pre-registered diagnostic — the direct measurement of T3's claimed mechanism — decomposes each arm's error at \(n^\ast\) into approximation, the error still present at \(n_{\max} = 4096\) (read under the empty-VS rule where the version space empties), plus estimation, the per-target difference \(\mathrm{err}(n^\ast) - \mathrm{err}(n_{\max})\), in both weightings. Measured (w-fun medians), the two arms have the anatomy the theory assigns them. The left arm is approximation-driven: at \(k{=}1\) the approximation arm is \(0.250\) against an estimation share of \(0.022\) — per-target best-1-junta floors of \(1/4\) or \(1/2\), with empirical mean \(0.315\) against the anchor expectation \(c_{2,1} = 0.3\) of \eqref{eq:floor}, which the analysis re-verified exactly over the essential class (10 essential two-bit tables, mean best-1-junta error \(0.300\)). The right arm is estimation-driven: for \(k \ge r\) the class contains the target and the approximation arm vanishes (median \(\mathrm{err}(n_{\max}) = 0.000\) at \(k = 2, 3, 4\)), so the excess at \(n^\ast\) — \(0.090,\ 0.237,\ 0.316\) — is entirely finite-sample: the spurious-consistency effect the corollary of §3.3 attributes to the uniform-VS predictor specifically, the effect a deterministic Occam-ERM collapses exactly. The same anatomy holds at \(r{=}3\) (approximation \(0.250\) vs estimation \(0.089\) at \(k{=}2\); \(0.000\) vs \(0.249,\ 0.349\) at \(k = 3, 4\)) and under w-rep. The pre-registered numeric probe for T3′ (the right arm as a theorem) found 97.7% of paired \((k \to k{+}1)\) comparisons monotone — suggestive, but the theorem was not attempted, per the pre-commitment.

6. Controls

P4 — basis-mixing (gating for the mechanism story). The class is defined in a basis (§2), so composing each target with a random invertible GF(2) linear map should move it out of the axis-aligned class and erase the advantage — if it does not, the axis-alignment story is wrong. One map per target at \((r{=}2, d{=}9, k{=}2)\); the mixed version space is typically empty, so the learner is the uniform-min-ERM predictor (§2). Clauses: median mixed error at \(n^\ast\) at least \(0.25\), against the unmixed median at most \(0.10\). Result: pass. Median error \(0.404\) mixed vs \(0.090\) unmixed. Reported distributionally (Figure 4): the pre-registered decomposition separates approximation from estimation via the post-mixing best-in-class error \(\inf_{h\in\mathcal F_{k=2}}\mathrm{err}(h)\), computed per target in closed form — it is \(0.25\) for 15/20 targets and \(0.5\) for the other 5, so the collapse is approximation-driven: the target genuinely leaves the class. The fraction of mixed targets coincidentally re-entering a low-arity axis-aligned class (best-in-class error below \(0.10\)) is \(0/20\).

Label-shuffle smoke. With labels resampled independently per distinct training input (keeping L-flat single-valued) and error still measured against the true function, every learner should sit at chance at \(n = 512\): pass band \(0.5 \pm 0.05\). All three do — medians \(0.501\) (L-flat), \(0.496\) (L-known), \(0.504\) (L-unk) — so nothing in the pipeline leaks labels. The campaign's anchor re-verification (\(c_{2,1} = 0.300\) exactly, §5.2) doubles as a correctness check of the closed-form junta machinery.

7. Mechanism

M1/P5 — the counting engine, made visible (descriptive). Every finite-class bound above runs on one quantity: how fast the version space shrinks. The campaign measures \(\log_2|\text{version space}|\) directly (min-disagreement set when empty). At \(n^\ast\), the pre-registered strict ordering holds: \(0.13\) (L-known) \(\lt\) \(0.84\) (L-unk) \(\lt\) \(500.0\) (L-flat) — Figure 5, which renders M1 as \(\log_2|\text{VS}|\) vs \(n\): M1's pre-registered rendering, “plotted against error”, is delivered numerically — this ordering and the slope check at \(n^\ast\) — rather than as an error-vs-\(|\text{VS}|\) panel. P5's second clause, verbatim “shrinkage slope within \(2\times\) of the counting prediction”, is instantiated as a value-at-\(n^\ast\) agreement on the flat arm (the one with an exact counting prediction): \(\log_2|\text{VS}|\) starts at the known \(2^{d} = 512\), so the value at \(n^\ast\) fixes the average slope over \([0, n^\ast]\), and the measured \(500.0\) against the analytic \(2^{d}(1 - 2^{-d})^{n^\ast} = 500.13\) is a slope ratio of \(1.01\) — well within the \(2\times\) band — tying the measured curves back to the counting arguments of §3.

P2 — the graph-selection gap (pre-registered consequence, non-gating). T2's two-term structure predicts a positive, slowly growing cost for not knowing the wiring: \(\mathrm{gap}_i(d) = n_{i,\text{L-unk}}(0.1; d) - n_{i,\text{L-known}}(0.1; d)\). Clauses: at each \(d \in \{6,9,12\}\), median gap positive with one-sided sign test \(p \lt 0.05\); growth, \(\mathrm{median}[\mathrm{gap}(12)]\) between \(1\times\) and \(2.5\times\) \(\mathrm{median}[\mathrm{gap}(6)]\) (log-like, not polynomial). Result: positivity holds; growth is not resolvable at these scales. L-known's crossings are left-censored at \(n_{\min} = 8\) for 20/20 targets at every \(d\) (a 16-entry table class pins down almost immediately), so per the censoring rules the gaps enter as conservative lower bounds \(\mathrm{gap}_i \gt n_{i,\text{L-unk}} - 8\): their medians are \(1.9,\ 3.7,\ 4.8\) samples at \(d = 6, 9, 12\) (CIs \([1.5, 2.4]\), \([2.8, 4.1]\), \([4.4, 5.3]\); all 20 targets positive at each \(d\), sign tests \(p = 2^{-20} \approx 9.5\times10^{-7}\); Figure 6, left). The growth clauses cannot be decided on lower bounds, so P2 as a whole takes its pre-registered non-fatal downgrade: T2's graph term is positive but not separately resolvable at these scales beyond positivity.

M2 — additivity of the two costs (descriptive). On the \(N{=}2\) chain, the complementary oracles decompose the total cost: at \(d{=}6\), median \(n_{\text{L-known}}(0.1) + n_{\text{L-tables-oracle}}(0.1) = 9.7 + 9.8 = 19.5\) vs \(n_{\text{L-unk}}(0.1) = 16.9\) (ratio \(1.16\)); at \(d{=}9\), \(9.7 + 13.1 = 22.8\) vs \(20.7\) (ratio \(1.10\)) — both within the pre-registered descriptive band of \(1.5\times\) (Figure 6, right). Table-learning and graph-selection costs superpose approximately additively, as the two-term bound \eqref{eq:t2} suggests they should. One scope note carries from §4: the chain's L-unk knows the wiring shape — a narrowing, recorded at run time rather than pre-registered, of the locked learner spec's full fan-in-\(r\) structure class — so its graph-selection component measures input-assignment selection under a known shape, and the additivity reading carries that scope.

9. Scope and limitations

What the theorems do not say. Per-neuron fan-in does not imply module-level fan-in — the plan's own pre-registered guard, quoted verbatim from the intake: “A module with many neurons can collectively read many upstream variables even if every scalar neuron only reads (k) inputs. So per-neuron fan-in does not mathematically imply module fan-in.” All statements are hypothesis-class statements: SGD-trained fan-in-limited MLPs are an explicitly deferred successor study, and nothing here validates their behavior. Dense networks can represent everything in this note — the claim is statistical complexity (inductive bias), never expressivity. And the advantage is class-membership relative to a basis: under GF(2) mixing the target leaves the axis-aligned class and the advantage must — and does (§6) — vanish. This is consonant with the foundations node: there the representation varies with the class fixed; here the class membership itself is basis-relative.

Open, by declaration rather than oversight: the \(N\)-linear term of T1/T2 is not matched from below (T4 forces only the \(N{=}1\) subclass's \(2^{r} + r\log(d/r)\)); realizability at \(N \ge 2\), \(2 \le k \lt r\) is the floor's declared open case; and T3′ — the VS right arm as a theorem — was left unattempted under its pre-commitment, with a 97.7%-monotone numeric probe on record. The capacity separation holds under the explicit regime \(\Delta = O(r D^{r-1})\) with the dense side counted by descriptions; the lower bound is claimed at \(D{=}2\) only.

Empirically, exact enumeration buys exactness and pays reach: \(d \le 12\), description counts under \(5\times10^{7}\), and a grid floor \(n_{\min} = 8\) that left-censors the known-structure baselines — which is why P2's growth clause and P6b could not resolve (§7, §5.1); the \(D^{r}\) magnitude check P7 ran at a single small scale and landed below its band (§5.3). These are scale limits of the illustration, not of the theorems.
